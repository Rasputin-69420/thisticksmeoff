Model Details — Thermodynamic Civilization Simulator v70.2 (Emergent)

Core Premise

The productive economy functions like a heat engine because competition and market discipline force resources to be converted into useful output efficiently — exactly like a large temperature gradient drives a Carnot engine.

Some of that output is used to run essential services through government institutions. However, no engine is 100% efficient. Waste heat inevitably leaks into the government/bureaucracy side (the cold reservoir). Over time this causes the cold side to warm up: bureaucracy grows, institutions become more complex and less effective, and the temperature difference that powers the engine shrinks. As a result, the system generates less real useful work.

When efficiency drops, governments usually respond by trying to force preferred outcomes anyway (equality, equity, redistribution) through heavy political direction. In the model this is represented as running a heat pump — actively moving resources against the natural gradient. This requires extra energy and increases overall entropy, effectively violating the Second Law. The civilization ends up spending more and more of its productive capacity just to fight disorder instead of creating surplus, until the engine can no longer sustain the growing cold reservoir and the system risks collapse.
