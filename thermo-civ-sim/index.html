Thermodynamic Civilization Model

A Carnot Engine Framework for Understanding
Societal Productivity, Governance, and Entropy

Version 1.0 — June 2026

1. Core Philosophy & Thermodynamic Analogy

Hot Reservoir — Productive sector (builders, entrepreneurs, competitive firms, engineers). Source of value creation and useful work under market discipline.

Cold Reservoir — Bureaucratic / governmental sink (administrative overhead, non-competitive allocation, political processes).

Heat Engine (Carnot-like) — Activities that remain subject to competitive pressure and profit/loss discipline. These approximate maximum efficiency between the two reservoirs.

Heat Leaks → Heat Pump Transition — When leaks from the productive sector into pure bureaucracy become self-reinforcing through political mechanisms, the cold reservoir begins acting as an active heat pump, extracting work from the hot reservoir while producing net entropy.

Key Principle — No configuration can produce net positive work by running a less efficient process to "correct" a more efficient one without external work input and net entropy increase (second law constraint).

2. Core Variables

  • TH(t) — Effective temperature of productive (hot) reservoir
  • TC(t) — Effective temperature of bureaucratic (cold) reservoir
  • K(t) — Productive capital stock
  • Saccum(t) — Accumulated entropy stock (latent irreversibility)
  • S*(t) — Dynamic entropy threshold

3. Policy Molecule Domains

There are 7 domains. Each contains particles with state: velocity vi, mass mi, discretionary power di, accountability ai.

Domain 1: Fiscal Extraction & Allocation — Tax structure, transfers, budget allocation rules.

Domain 2: Procurement & Contracting — Competitive vs. politically directed procurement share, contractor accountability.

Domain 3: Regulatory & Permitting — Business regulatory burden, licensing, entry barriers.

Domain 4: Monetary & Credit System — Money creation rules, bailout expectations, credit allocation.

Domain 5: Education & Credentialing — Compulsory schooling, curriculum control, credentialing linkage.

Domain 6: Information Environment & Narrative Infrastructure (highest leverage) — Media structure, public messaging, platform rules.

Domain 7: Infrastructure & Essential Services Delivery — Competitive vs. tax-funded mode, maintenance discipline.

4. Agent Self-Enrichment Incentive (ASEI)

Cross-cutting mechanism.

ASEIi = f ( di / (ai + ε) )

where f is monotonically increasing. High ASEI amplifies entropy production on particles with high discretionary power and low accountability. Particularly strong in Domains 2, 3, 4, and 6.

5. Composite Economic Temperature Teff

Teff(t) = ( ∏k=1 to 6 wk · Tk(t) ) 1 / Σ wk

Six Sub-Indices Tk (FeedbackLatency, SkinInGame, InformationFidelity, ErrorExtinction, ReallocationFluidity, CoercionIntensity) combine into overall system efficiency.

6. Entropy Production

Relative entropy production rate:

Ṡrel(t) = 1/K(t) · (α ΔSflow + β ΔSalloc + γ ΔSmol)

Flow Entropy

ΔSflow = Σ mi · Li · (1/TC − 1/TH) · (1 + wASEI · ASEIi)

Allocation Irreversibility

ΔSalloc = Σ mi · ((Deadweighti + Persistencei) / Teff(t)) · (1 + wASEI · ASEIi)

7. Entropy Accumulation, Masking & Threshold

Accumulation

dSaccum/dt = Ṡrel(t) − M(t)

Masking Rate

M(t) = m0 + mmonetary · Pmonetary(t) + mnarrative · Cmedia(t) · ASEImedia

Masking (especially monetary and narrative) can temporarily slow the rise of Saccum.

8. Breaking Event Types

Breaking events are nonlinear corrections when the threshold is crossed. Type and severity depend on which domains have highest ASEI and entropy contribution.

Type A: Monetary / Credit Regime Break — Sharp drop in information fidelity, sudden visibility of masked misallocations, temporary drop in TH, partial reduction in Saccum.

Type B: Narrative / Legitimacy Break — Collapse of media clustering factor, sudden improvement in information fidelity, reduction in heat-pump power.

Type C: Procurement / Contracting Accountability Break — Forced increase in accountability rules, rise in error extinction rate, reduction in politically directed procurement mass.

Type D: Regulatory Gridlock Break — Increase in reallocation fluidity, reduction in feedback latency, possible forced regulatory sunsets.

Type E: Broad Capital / Productivity Collapse — Direct reduction in K(t), broad drop in TH, largest one-time reduction in Saccum.
